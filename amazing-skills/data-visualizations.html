Data Visualizations

For synthesis one, OkCupid Drug Data, I created donut-style pie charts to visualize demographic data of OkCupid users that did or did not use drugs. The main challenge was this data was the enormous gap in participants between the categories; there were 410 people who did drugs often, 7732 people who did drugs sometimes, and 37725 people who never did drugs.

One of my group members suggested that I should represent the numbers as total amounts with bar graphs, rather than percentages with pie charts. I disagreed. I think this would be a bad idea beacause it would make the numbers incomparable. It would work well for comparing answers within the same categories, but it would be impossible to compare different categories.
