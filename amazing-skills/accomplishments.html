Accomplishments

For synthesis two, The Evolution of Chance, I created text documents for every Chance the Rapper song and built a Python program, The Chano-Meter, to automatically count the number of occurances of any given word in every song and album. Manipulating this huge dataset - 41 songs with about 300 words each, approximately 12,300 words total - made something completely incomprehensible easy to understand with a simple look. Furthermore, my program collected data on entire albums as well as individual songs.

While this program gathered a lot of information, there was a decent amount of data lost in the process. Especially considering Chance is a hip hop artist, every song contained a myriad of synonyms, slang words, double entendres, and other types of wordplay that were unable to be picked up by my simple one-word search process. I did create some theme sets to group similar words together to combat this; for example, I created one chart visualizing all the occurances of the words “killed”, “murdered”, “death”, “died”, “shot”, or “shooting” and one for the words “God”, “Lord”, and “Jesus”.

However, creating theme categories was only a partial fix. The lack of complete themes became an especially big problem when I wanted to create a chart for words referring to drugs - I quickly realized that there were just too many types of drugs and different street names to count them all. With more resources, I would have used an established database of word categories to guide the project.
